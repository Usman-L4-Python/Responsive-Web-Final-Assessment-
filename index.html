Our History

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates (UAE), has a long and rich history. People have lived in the area for thousands of years, with early communities depending on fishing, pearling, and trade. The Al Nahyan family, which rules Abu Dhabi today, established its power in the 18th century.In the 1950s, the discovery of oil changed everything. Oil wealth allowed the city to grow rapidly, turning it into a modern and thriving city. In 1971, Abu Dhabi became part of the United Arab Emirates, a new country made up of seven emirates. Today, Abu Dhabi is an important city in the UAE and the world.

Our Tradition

The traditions of Abu Dhabi are rooted in its Bedouin heritage, with customs that emphasize hospitality, respect for elders, and pride in family. Traditional crafts such as weaving, pottery, and pearl diving are still celebrated, and many Emiratis continue to follow their ancestors' way of life while embracing modernity.
